About Me

Career Highlight:

  • Post-Doctoral Research Associate at Purdue University
  • Multiphase flows
  • Drops and bubbles
  • Wetting and adhesion
  • Pore-scale fluid mechanics
  • Microscale heat transfer

I am a Post-Doctoral Research Associate in the School of Mechanical Engineering of Purdue University, currently researching and developing next-generation thermal management solutions for power electronics devices and data centers under the supervision of Prof. Justin A. Weibel. I am focused on developing sustainable solutions that enable efficient heat dissipation from power-dense sources while reducing operational energy usage and carbon footprint. I have effectively used my prior experience in experimental/theoretical fluid mechanics, two-phase heat and mass transfer across scales, porous media transport properties, and surface science/engineering to develop thermal management solutions. I have been using numerical simulations, experimental methods, and mathematical modelling to explore effective thermal management solutions.

Previously, I earned my doctoral degree from the Department of Mechanical Engineering of the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, under the joint supervision of Prof. Amaresh Dalal and Prof. Dipankar Narayan Basu in February 2021. My doctoral research focuses on understanding the dynamics of liquid drops, their deformation, and fragmentation in the various potential fields such as electric field, magnetic field, and gravitational field catalyzed by the substrate wettability and adhesion behavior (further details on research and publications page). While I have mostly resorted to experimental realizations, theory and numerical simulations have been performed for complementing the critical observations from the experiments. Prior to that, I graduated with B. Tech degree in Mechanical Engineering from the National Institute of Technology, Silchar, India, in 2013. Post-graduation, I worked with Maruti Suzuki India Limited as a research and development engineer in the diesel engine division from 2013 to 2015

I I enjoy working with high-speed cameras and cherish unrevealing the intricate details of fluid flow phenomena with its help. Currently, I have been exploring the effect of wettability in the microscale heat transfer phenomena. I also have developed an image processing code, useful for high-speed videography techniques during my doctoral studies. I also have gained hands-on experience in coding, specifically in C/C++ language (and a little bit of python!). I am passionate about teaching and outreach, specifically in science communication. I have also volunteered for many social activities, mostly for the betterment of the school students (More details in the outreach section).
