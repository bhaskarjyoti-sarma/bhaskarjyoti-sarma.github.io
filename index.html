About Me

I am a Post Doctoral Research Associate in the School of Mechanical Engineering of Purdue University, currently working in the broad area of "Microscale Fluid Flow and Heat Transfer" under the supervision of Prof. Justin A. Weibel. Prior to that I earned my doctoral degree from the Department of Mechanical Engineering in Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, under the joint supervision of Dr Amaresh Dalal and Dr. Dipankar Narayan Basu on February, 2021. My Ph.D. work primarily focuses on understanding the dynamics of liquid drops, its deformation, and fragmentation in the various potential fields such as electric field, magnetic field, and gravitational field (further details on research and publications page) with the help of experiments, theory, and simulation. I enjoy working with high-speed cameras and cherish unrevealing the intricate details of fluid flow phenomena with the help of it. Currently, I have started taking an interest in active matter. I also have developed a small image processing code, useful for high-speed videography techniques. I also have gained hands on experience in coding, specifically in C/C++ language (and a little bit of python!).

I graduated with a B. Tech in Mechanical Engineering from National Institute of Technology, Silchar, India, in 2013. Post-graduation, I worked with Maruti Suzuki India Limited as a research and development engineer in the diesel engine division from 2013 to 2015. Before joining IIT Guwhati for Ph.D, I had also worked as a junior research fellow in the "Anupravaha" project with Dr. Amaresh Dalal for six months in 2015. I am passionate about teaching and outreach, specifically in science communication. I have also volunteered for many social activities, mostly for the betterment of the school students.(More details in the outreach section)
